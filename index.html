M.C. Escher

Here's a time line of M.C. Eschers Life:

  • 1898 - Maurits Cornelis Escher born on June 17th in Leeuwarden, Netherlands.
  • 1919-1922 - Attends Haarlem School of Architecture and Decorative Arts, shifts focus from architecture to graphic arts encouraged by his teacher Samuel Jessurun de Mesquita.
  • 1922-1935 - Lives in Italy, traveling and sketching throughout the country during the spring and summer.
  • 1924 - Marries Jetta Umiker and they make their home in Frascati, outside of Rome.
  • 1926 - Their first child, George, is born.
  • 1927-1935 - Almost every year, Escher makes a mountain trip through desolate regions, sketches he will use later on for his work, he also takes a lot of photos and keeps a travel diary.
  • 1928 - Their second child, Arthur, is born.
  • 1929 - Escher first learns to make lithographs.
  • 1935 - The family leaves Italy due to the rise of Fascism and moves to Chateau-d'Oex in Switzerland.
  • 1936 - Visits the Alhambra in Spain, which inspires his tessellations and geometric patterns.
  • 1938 - Their third child, Jan, is born.
  • 1950's - His work gains widespread international recognition, including articles in Time and Life magazines.
  • 1951 - Two articles from 1951, published in Time and Life, featured his work and made him famous in America.
  • 1953 - Creates "Relativity'.
  • 1960 - A book of Escher's prints is published and brings him fame in Canada and Russia, and recognition from the international scientific community.
  • 1963 - Creates "Mobius Strip II (Red Ants)".
  • 1969 - Creates his last major piece, "Snakes".
  • 1972 - dies at the age of 73.

"It is in the nature of people to want to exchange ideas with each other. And I believe that, basically, every artist wants nothing more than to tell his fellow people what he has in mind. With all expressions of art, whether it is music, literature or visual arts, it is first and foremost a matter of: communicating to the outside world and make sensory perceptible a personal thought, a striking idea or an inner emotion."

-- MC Escher
